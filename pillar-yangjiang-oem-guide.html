Key Takeaway / At a Glance

Yangjiang offers unmatched scale, flexibility, and cost efficiency for knife OEM. Quality ranges from disposable-grade to premium export-standard depending on factory tier. The gap between a top-tier Yangjiang factory and a bottom-tier workshop is larger than the gap between a mid-tier Chinese factory and a German one. This variability is both the risk and the opportunity of sourcing from Yangjiang.

Yangjiang Industry Overview

Understanding the structure of Yangjiang's knife industry helps buyers navigate partner selection and set realistic expectations.

1

Geographic Clustering

Yangjiang's knife industry is concentrated in several districts, each with a slightly different specialization:

  • Jiangcheng District: Large export-oriented manufacturers with full in-house capabilities (forging, heat treatment, grinding, assembly, packaging).
  • Yangdong District: Mid-sized factories and trading companies. Many serve as intermediaries between small workshops and international buyers.
  • Surrounding towns: Thousands of small workshops producing components (blanks, handles, sheaths) or assembling finished goods from outsourced parts.
2

Supply Chain Depth

Yangjiang's advantage is not just labor cost — it is vertical integration. Within a 50km radius, you can source:

  • Raw steel sheets and bars (domestic and imported grades)
  • Forging and stamping services
  • Heat-treatment facilities (some shared, some in-house)
  • Handle materials (wood, G-10, micarta, pakka, PP/TPE)
  • Packaging (retail boxes, magnetic closures, blister packs)
  • Logistics and export documentation services
3

Export Orientation

Top-tier Yangjiang factories export primarily to:

  • North America: Retail chains, Amazon brands, TV shopping networks
  • Europe: Supermarket private label, kitchenware distributors
  • Japan: Mid-tier kitchen knives and scissors under Japanese brand licenses
  • Middle East & Southeast Asia: Volume kitchen sets and outdoor knives

Factory Tier System

Yangjiang factories fall into three broad tiers. Knowing which tier matches your program saves time and prevents mismatched expectations.

Tier 1 — Export Brands

Top Tier

200+ workers, in-house R&D, ISO certification, direct export experience with major Western brands. Capable of premium steels (VG-10, 14C28N, Damascus), tight tolerances, and full compliance documentation.

MOQ: 1,000–3,000 | Lead: 45–75 days

Tier 2 — Mid-Size Manufacturers

Mid Tier

50–200 workers, mixed in-house and outsourced processes. Good for standard stainless steels (5Cr15MoV, 7Cr17MoV) and mainstream designs. Often willing to work with smaller MOQs for growing brands.

MOQ: 500–1,500 | Lead: 30–60 days

Tier 3 — Workshops & Trading Co.

Entry

10–50 workers, often assembly-only with outsourced blades and handles. Lowest costs but highest variability. Suitable only for entry-level, price-sensitive programs with loose tolerances.

MOQ: 300–800 | Lead: 20–45 days

Tier is not permanent. A Tier 2 factory investing in new heat-treatment equipment may outperform a stagnant Tier 1. Conversely, a Tier 1 factory accepting too many low-margin orders may degrade QC. Audit current capabilities, not historical reputation.

MOQ, Lead Time & Certification

Negotiating terms with Yangjiang factories requires understanding their cost structure and what drives minimums and timelines.

MOQ Drivers

300 – 3,000 units

MOQ is driven by steel procurement minimums, heat-treatment batch sizes, and assembly-line setup costs. Mixed-SKU orders can sometimes share MOQ if steel grade and handle type are consistent.

Lead Time Drivers

20 – 90 days

Standard steels: 30–45 days. Premium/imported steels: 45–75 days (steel procurement adds 1–3 weeks). Complex packaging or new molds: +2–4 weeks. Peak season (Q3–Q4): add 1–2 weeks.

Certification Costs

$500 – $3,000

FDA food-contact: usually included or minimal cost. LFGB: $500–$1,500 depending on test scope. ISO 8442: factory-level cert, rarely per-product. Third-party audit (BSCI/Sedex): $2,000–$5,000.

Sample Costs

$50 – $300

Pre-production samples typically cost 2–5x the per-unit FOB price. Some factories credit sample costs against the first order. Always request a pre-production sample before approving bulk.

Certification Checklist for Yangjiang Sourcing

FDA compliance: Required for US food-contact sales. Request test reports or material declarations.
LFGB / EU 1935/2004: Required for EU market entry. Confirm the factory has recent LFGB test reports for similar products.
ISO 8442: General knife safety standard. Useful but not mandatory in all markets.
Prop 65 (California): If selling in California, ensure handle materials and coatings comply with Prop 65 disclosure requirements.
REACH: For EU sales, confirm coatings, dyes, and handle materials do not contain restricted substances.

Quality Control & Factory Audits

Quality in Yangjiang is not a matter of geography — it is a matter of process control. Here is how to enforce it.

1

Pre-Production Verification

Before any bulk production begins, verify the following:

  • Steel certificate: Request mill test certificate (MTC) or distributor certificate for the steel lot being used.
  • Pre-production sample: Evaluate geometry, weight, balance, edge sharpness, and finish against your spec sheet.
  • Heat-treatment protocol: Confirm target HRC, quenching medium, and tempering schedule. Request a sample hardness test report.
  • Handle material cert: For food-contact handles, confirm material grade and any required migration testing.
2

In-Process Inspection

For first orders or new factories, consider in-process checks:

  • Post-heat-treatment: Random-sample hardness testing on blade blanks before grinding.
  • Post-grind: Check bevel symmetry, edge geometry consistency, and surface finish.
  • Pre-assembly: Verify handle fit, scale alignment, and hardware torque.
  • Final QA: Edge sharpness test, blade centering (for folders), lock engagement (for folders), and cosmetic inspection.
3

Pre-Shipment Inspection (PSI)

A PSI by a third party (SGS, Bureau Veritas, or local inspection agencies) is standard practice for container-load orders:

  • Random sampling per AQL 2.5 or agreed standard
  • Functional testing: edge retention sample, handle strength, corrosion spot check
  • Packaging and labeling verification
  • Carton drop test for transport durability

Do not skip the PSI. Even top-tier factories can have batch issues. A $300–$500 PSI can prevent a container of defective goods from reaching your warehouse. For new factories, consider a 100% inspection on the first order.

Common Pitfalls & Red Flags

After 15 years of Yangjiang manufacturing execution, these are the most common and costly mistakes we see buyers make.

Red Flag: Vague Steel Claims

Critical

Factories claiming "German steel," "Japanese steel," or "equivalent to VG-10" without certificates. Always specify the exact grade and request documentation.

Red Flag: Price Too Good

Critical

If a quote is 30%+ below comparable factories, investigate carefully. Common shortcuts: substandard steel substitutions, skipped tempering steps, or thinner blade stock than specified.

Pitfall: Ignoring Heat Treatment

Common

Many buyers specify steel grade but not HRC target or heat-treatment protocol. A good steel with poor heat treatment performs worse than a budget steel with optimal heat treatment.

Pitfall: Skipping the Trial Order

Common

Placing a large first order with an unaudited factory is high-risk. Start with 300–500 units to validate consistency, communication, and QC before scaling.

Pitfall: Confusing Trading Co. with Factory

Common

Many Yangjiang "manufacturers" are trading companies with no in-house production. They add margin and reduce QC control. Verify production capacity during audit.

Pitfall: Inadequate Spec Sheets

Common

A one-page PO with only blade length and steel grade is insufficient. Include: HRC target, bevel angle, handle material, weight range, finish spec, packaging requirements, and acceptance criteria.

Partner Vetting Checklist

Request business license and export license verification
Ask for 2–3 customer references with contactable buyers
Verify in-house capabilities (forging, heat treatment, grinding, assembly)
Request recent steel certificates and hardness test reports
Audit the factory in person or via third-party inspection
Start with a small trial order before committing to volume

Frequently Asked Questions

For End Users

What is Yangjiang known for?
Yangjiang is one of the world's largest knife and scissors manufacturing centers, producing an estimated 40%+ of global kitchen and EDC knife volume. It has over 1,500 factories and a blade-making history dating back over 1,500 years.
Are Yangjiang knives good quality?
Quality varies enormously by factory. Top-tier Yangjiang factories produce knives that match or exceed mid-tier Western brands in fit and finish. Low-tier workshops produce disposable-grade goods. The key is partner selection and specification — not geography.
Can I buy directly from Yangjiang as a consumer?
Direct factory access is generally restricted to B2B buyers with MOQs. Consumers typically buy through brands, distributors, or platforms like Alibaba. Be cautious of unbranded "factory direct" claims on consumer marketplaces.

For B2B Buyers

What is the typical MOQ from a Yangjiang factory?
Standard MOQs range from 300 to 1,000 pieces per SKU for mainstream steels. Premium steels or complex designs may require 1,000+. Some mid-tier factories accept 300-unit first orders to build relationships.
How do I avoid low-quality factories in Yangjiang?
Request steel certificates, hardness reports, and customer references. Audit the factory or hire a third-party inspector. Start with a trial order. Avoid factories that refuse documentation, offer prices far below market, or claim vague "equivalent" steels.
Can Yangjiang factories make premium steels like VG-10 or Damascus?
Yes. Top-tier Yangjiang factories regularly work with VG-10, 14C28N, 9Cr18MoV, and layered Damascus. However, not every factory has experience with these grades. Verify past production and request samples before committing.
What certifications can Yangjiang factories provide?
Most export-oriented factories can support FDA (US), LFGB (EU), ISO 8442, and BSCI/Sedex audits. Confirm specific certification availability before selecting a partner, as capabilities vary by factory tier.
How important is heat treatment when sourcing from China?
Heat treatment is the single most important quality variable. A well-heat-treated 5Cr15MoV can outperform a poorly heat-treated 9Cr18MoV. Always specify target HRC and request batch hardness documentation.
Should I visit Yangjiang in person?
A personal visit is highly recommended for programs over $50,000 or for long-term partnerships. If travel is not feasible, hire a reputable third-party audit firm. Video factory tours are helpful but not a substitute for physical inspection.
